How we met

We were but two quiet little dental school nerds who sat side by side in the front row of class for years, comparing notes, work, and eventually some music and crossword puzzles. As time went by, we both realized how much we shared and how close we had grown.

The Proposal: Her

Our entire relationship has been punctuated by music. Yes, we spent our early months as friends sharing notes and lecture series but my first clear memory of an interaction with Bryan (was after he cut his hair). It was one of those gorgeous early spring days when the midday temperature just hit 60 degrees and it felt hot on skin that was used to a 32 degree winter. I was coming back to our school building when I saw him sitting outside listening to music while studying. I parked myself right next to him, pulled one of my own earphones out of my ear and stuck it in his so he could hear what I was listening to.
The band was Sunset Rubdown, and the song was “The Mending of the Gown.” We hardly spoke, moved our heads in sync to the music and reviewed notes. And that was truly the beginning.
So it was only appropriate that our engagement weekend was a celebration of music as well – in Nashville – the city of music. Except that I only knew this weekend as the surprise birthday weekend I had planned for Bryan – not one of the largest milestones in our relationship. Our first evening would be just for the two of us – as the next few days his family members would start trickling in to celebrate his big birthday. He did not know any of this at the time either!! We spent the night in the city as we usually do when we travel – casually walking and exploring with a camera and some shared earphones. To note, we had actually created a playlist for the weekend and were listening to it at the time. We wandered over the pedestrian bridge onto a private park that overlooked downtown Broadway – whose lights were reflecting off the river into the dark night sky. It was definitely photo worthy.
Bryan spent awhile trying to line up his photo frame while I was spotting his drone, listening to music and dancing. Finally it was time to take our photo and he pulled me onto some rocks lining the river. The song playing was “I Don’t Owe You a Thing” by Gary Clark Jr. I then actually tried to go stand on the same rock he was on – he pushed me off back onto my own rock, held my hands and started telling me how much I mean to him.
You have to keep in mind, we’ve been dating for a while – so it’s not unusual for us to share these kinds of sentiments with each other and I just assumed it was another beautiful moment we were enjoying in each other’s company. Moments later, he was down on one knee, opening a box in his hand and asking me to be his partner in life – his wife. I can honestly say despite how long we’ve been dating, he was still able to surprise me. After our moment, and rapidly packing up all of our photography supplies, the ear phones went back to Gary Clark Jr and his perfectly timed lyrics of “I don’t owe you a thing, we ain’t getting married, I ain’t buying you no diamond ring.” We couldn’t help but laugh at the irony.

The Proposal: Him

Bryan: Alex had swept me away on a romantic surprise birthday to Nashville. We absolutely love Nashville for its music, people, and its support of both preservation and growth of culture. What she didn't know was that after months of trying to plan a perfect and unexpected proposal, I realized that a surprise meant for me was the best cover. The first night we arrived was beautiful, and we decided to take a stroll around the waterfront and bridges so that I could do some photography. It was my plan to use this to do some scouting for a perfect proposal site. When we came to a spot on a small pier that looked across the water at the neon of Broadway, I knew it was there. What I didn't know was that it was NOW. I had come prepared and had the ring with me (just in case), but neither of us was dressed for the occasion. For anyone who knows us, this made the moment even more appropriate. Alex was dancing to some music on the pier waiting for me to have her pose for what she didn't know would be our proposal photos. We stood hand in hand waiting for the camera to indicate it was ready while I shared with her all the she has meant and means to me. I don't think it was until I took a knee with ring in hand that she was aware of just how much I truly meant what I was saying. There was a bunch of hugging, kissing, and happy tears that started one of the best nights of my life!

Wedding Venue

We wanted to come together as a couple in a place that represented us as a couple. He's a touch raw and Southern. She's a bit city and chic. From the moment we set foot on the grounds at Blue Hill it felt right. Blue Hill at Stone Barns is at its heart a farm, its crops and livestock are its roots. And from those roots rose a love and appreciation of the elements and how with proper handling, they produce something refined. We love how it takes what it has at hand, and turns it into something amazing.
